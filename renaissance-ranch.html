What the audit found

  • No location pages — you have 14+ facilities but zero pages targeting searches like "drug rehab Brigham City" or "alcohol recovery Sandy." Competitors with one location outrank you because they have a dedicated page.
  • Site hasn't been updated in over a year — almost every page was last modified March 2024. Google deprioritizes stale content.
  • WordPress + Divi is slow — heavy theme patched with WP Rocket. Mobile load times are poor. Page speed affects both rankings and whether families stay on the site.
  • No AEO (answer engine optimization) — when someone asks ChatGPT or Gemini "best Christian rehab in Utah," your site isn't structured to be cited. No schema markup, no structured data.
  • Generic content — the SEO agency output doesn't reflect your clinical expertise or faith-based approach. It reads like any other rehab site.
  • No live intake after hours — leads go to a Contact Form 7. No chat, no bot, no way to engage a family at 2am.

What you have that competitors don't

  • Faith-based positioning — Spirit Mountain, Alpine, and First Step can't authentically compete on Christian recovery. That's a search category you can own.
  • 14+ locations — each one is a keyword opportunity. 14 locations × 8 substance categories = 100+ unique pages no competitor can match.
  • 40+ clinical staff — real expertise that can be turned into authentic content. One recorded conversation produces months of material.

2. SEO — 100+ pages, indexed from launch

  • Location × substance matrix — 14 locations × 8 substances = 112 unique pages. Example: "Meth Recovery in Brigham City," "Alcohol Rehab in Sandy," "Fentanyl Treatment in Ogden." Each page targets a specific search query.

  • Technical SEO — proper meta titles and descriptions on every page, XML sitemap, internal linking structure, schema markup for local business + medical organization, image optimization, canonical URLs.
  • Blog framework — ongoing content system we populate weekly during consulting sessions. Fresh content signals to Google that the site is active.

3. AEO — structured for AI answer engines

  • What AEO is — when someone asks ChatGPT, Grok, or Gemini a question like "what's the best Christian rehab in Utah," the AI pulls its answer from websites. AEO is structuring your site so it gets cited.
  • Schema markup on every page — structured data that tells AI models exactly what each page contains: programs, locations, accreditations, reviews, FAQs.
  • FAQ sections — real questions families ask ("Do you take Medicaid?", "How long is the program?", "Is it faith-based?"), answered directly on the relevant pages. AI models use these as source material.
  • Entity clarity — the site clearly identifies Renaissance Ranch as a CARF-accredited, faith-based, multi-location treatment provider. AI models need this structured information to recommend you accurately.
  • Why this matters now — most rehab sites have zero AEO. The sites that structure their content first will be the ones AI engines default to recommending.

4. AI website assistant

  • What it is — a chatbot on your site that answers visitor questions about Renaissance Ranch 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
  • Trained on your programs — it knows your locations, services, insurance policies, admissions process, and faith-based approach. It gives accurate, specific answers — not generic responses.
  • Lead capture — when someone engages, the bot collects their name, contact info, substance concern, and urgency. That information is delivered to your admissions team automatically.
  • After-hours coverage — a family on your site at 2am gets a real conversation about your programs instead of a contact form. They get answers. You get their information.
