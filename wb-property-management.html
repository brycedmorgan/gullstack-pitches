What's missing today

  • No front door. The site skips straight to the AppFolio listings page. A prospective owner who just Googled "property manager Lehi" hits a rentals list with no idea who you are, what you offer, or why to call.
  • No lead capture. No form, no "Free Rental Analysis" CTA, no way to turn a visitor into an inquiry. Every click is a guess.
  • No social proof. No Google Reviews on-site, no testimonials, no staff photos, nothing that says "you can trust us with a six-figure asset."
  • No SEO footprint. Zero pages targeting the searches that matter — "property management Utah Valley," "short-term rental manager Provo," "rental manager Salt Lake City." Competitors with one location outrank you because they have content. You don't.
  • No portal prominence. Current and prospective residents/owners have to dig to find the login. It should be the most visible element in the header.
  • No story. The copy doesn't say you focus on rentals, that you can handle any property type, that you serve two valleys, or anything that sets WB apart from the franchises.

What you have going for you

  • AppFolio. Clean widget-based listings + portals. Same platform Bradley Properties uses — and it's the reason their site (the one you sent me) works. We plug into what you already pay for; no PMS switching.
  • Rentals-focused, full-capability. "We do rentals but can handle any property you own" is a better positioning than most PM sites. It qualifies the fit without closing the door.
  • Two-valley service area. Utah Valley + Salt Lake Valley is a real, searchable market. Every named city is a keyword opportunity.
  • A real lead magnet. The Utah Landlord Survival Guide is exactly the right top-of-funnel asset for your best-fit prospect — the private landlord who just realized they're in over their head.
  • A referral pipeline that's already working. Trent and Wes at Arrow of Light sent you to me. Owners talk to owners. A site that looks as competent as you are turns more of those referrals into booked calls.

The Build
Three phases. About three weeks to live.

Short, honest timeline. Each phase has a single checkpoint with you.

  • 45-minute call — walk through your voice, service area, positioning, what makes you different from the franchise PMs. Recorded.
  • Brand direction — colors, logo treatment, typography. If you have assets we use them; if not, we propose a palette and font pair for sign-off.

End of Week 1: direction signed off, copy approved, zero surprises.

Phase 2 — Build (Weeks 2–3)

  • All six pages built — custom-coded on a modern stack (Next.js on Vercel). Fast, responsive, accessible.

  • Google Reviews connected — live feed, auto-refreshing.
  • Lead forms wired — cycling lead magnet + rental analysis + contact form all route to your inbox. PDF auto-delivery configured.
  • Preview link shared — password-protected staging URL. You review, we tweak, nobody sees it but us until you say go.
End of Week 3: staging site reviewed, revisions in, ready for launch.

Phase 3 — Launch & go (Week 4)

  • Site goes live at whitmanburns.com (or wherever you want it pointed).
  • Google Business Profile optimized — NAP consistency, photos, services, Q&A, review template.
  • Search Console + Analytics wired — we set it up, you get login access.
  • Monthly maintenance + SEO begins (see below).
  • 30-day check-in — look at traffic, leads, what's ranking, what needs a nudge.
End of Week 4: live, indexed, capturing leads, and on a monthly rhythm.
